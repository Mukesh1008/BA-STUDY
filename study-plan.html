Q. Define Sonnet.

Answer: A sonnet is a short poem of fourteen lines. It has a specific rhyme scheme and structure. The Italian sonnet is divided into an octave and a sestet. The English or Shakespearean sonnet is divided into three quatrains and a final couplet.

Q. Write a short note on John Donne as a metaphysical poet.

Answer: John Donne is a leading figure of the metaphysical poets. His poetry is known for its intellectual and philosophical themes. He uses wit, irony, and complex arguments.

A key feature of his poetry is the 'metaphysical conceit'. A conceit is a comparison between two very different things. For example, in his poem "A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning", he compares two lovers' souls to the two legs of a compass. This is an unusual and surprising comparison. His poems often talk about themes like love, death, and religion in a very analytical and passionate way.

Model Answer: Section C (ЁЯФе High Priority)

Q. Discuss Shakespeare's 'Macbeth' as a tragedy of ambition.

Answer:

Introduction: Shakespeare's 'Macbeth' is a classic example of a tragedy. The play shows the downfall of a great and noble man, Macbeth. The main reason for his downfall is his "vaulting ambition," which means an excessive desire for power.

Main Points:

  1. Macbeth's Initial Character: At the start, Macbeth is a brave and loyal general. He is respected by King Duncan. However, the witches' prophecies awaken his hidden ambition to become king.
  2. Influence of Lady Macbeth: Lady Macbeth plays a crucial role. She encourages his ambition and questions his manhood to push him to kill King Duncan. She is more ambitious than Macbeth at the beginning.
  3. The First Murder: The murder of Duncan is the turning point. After this, Macbeth's ambition becomes a bloody path. To secure his throne, he commits more murders, including those of Banquo and Macduff's family.
  4. Guilt and Fear: His ambition brings him power, but not peace. He and Lady Macbeth suffer from guilt, sleeplessness, and paranoia. Macbeth sees Banquo's ghost, and Lady Macbeth sleepwalks. This shows that their ambition has destroyed their mental peace.
  5. Downfall and Death: In the end, Macbeth's ambition leads to his complete isolation and death. He becomes a tyrant feared by all. His story proves that unchecked ambition leads to destruction.

Conclusion: 'Macbeth' clearly shows that ambition, when it is not controlled by morals, can be a destructive force. It turns a hero into a villain and leads to his tragic end. The play serves as a powerful warning against the dangers of excessive ambition.
